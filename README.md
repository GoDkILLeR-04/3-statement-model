# 📊 Titan Company Limited — Three Statement Financial Model

## Overview
In order to evaluate past performance and project financial results over a five-year period, this project offers Titan Company Limited a fully integrated three-statement financial model.

To comprehend how operational assumptions affect profitability, liquidity, and overall financial sustainability, the model connects the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.

I developed this project as part of my shift to valuation modelling and financial analysis.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Build a structured three-statement financial model from scratch
- Understand financial statement linkages and accounting flow
- Forecast operating performance using realistic assumptions
- Analyze working capital dynamics and cash generation
- Create a foundation for valuation and investment analysis

---

## 🧱 Model Structure

### 1. Assumptions
Key operating drivers include:
- Revenue growth projections
- Cost structure assumptions
- Tax rate
- Working capital days (AR, Inventory, AP)
- Capital expenditure and depreciation
- Interest and financing assumptions

---

### 2. Income Statement
Projects:
- Revenue growth
- Cost of goods sold (COGS)
- Operating expenses
- Earnings before tax
- Net income

---

### 3. Balance Sheet
Tracks:
- Cash balances
- Working capital components
- Property & Equipment
- Debt and equity movements
- Retained earnings linkage

Balance sheet reconciliation ensures accounting consistency across periods.

---

### 4. Cash Flow Statement
Derived using the indirect method:
- Cash from operations
- Investing activities (CapEx)
- Financing activities
- Net change in cash

---

### 5. Supporting Schedules
- Working capital schedule
- Depreciation & PPE roll-forward
- Debt & interest schedule

---

## 📈 Forecast Methodology
Historical financial data was used to estimate operational trends. Forecasts are driven by assumption-based modeling rather than fixed outputs, allowing scenario analysis and sensitivity testing.

Key modeling principles:
- Statements fully linked
- No hard-coded forecast outputs
- Financial flows reconciled across statements
